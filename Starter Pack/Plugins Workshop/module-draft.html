What is a Value Metric?

A value metric is the unit you charge for. It should:

  • Align with how customers perceive value
  • Grow as customers get more value (natural expansion revenue)
  • Be easy to understand and predict

The Four Pricing Models

1. Per-User Pricing

Charge per seat/user. Examples: Slack ($8-15/user), Asana, Monday.com

Pros: Predictable, easy to understand, scales with team growth

Cons: Discourage s adding users, doesn't capture usage intensity

2. Usage-Based Pricing

Charge for consumption. Examples: AWS, Snowflake, Twilio

Pros: Fair (pay for what you use), natural expansion, no vendor lock-in fear

Cons: Unpredictable bills, complex to meter

3. Tiered Pricing

Offer Good/Better/Best packages with feature differentiation

Most common in SaaS - gives customers choice and clear upgrade path

4. Hybrid Models

Combine multiple metrics (e.g., Salesforce: users + tier)
